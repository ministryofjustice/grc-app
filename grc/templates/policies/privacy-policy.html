Privacy policy

This page explains what kind of personal information the Government Equalities Office (GEO) holds about you, how it's protected and how you can find out about it.

What personal information GEO holds about you

GEO hold the following information about you when you apply for a Gender Recognition Certificate using this service:

  • your personal details: name, email address, and telephone number
  • birth/adoption certificate details
  • Medical information
  • Name change information
  • National Insurance number

Purpose

We use your personal data to enable the submission of applications to the Gender Recognition team at HMCTS (HM Courts & Tribunals Service).

Legal basis of processing

  • Data subject consents

Sensitive personal data is personal data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, or trade union membership, and the processing of genetic data, biometric data for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, data concerning health or data concerning a natural person's sex life or sexual orientation.

The legal basis for processing your sensitive personal data is:

  • you have freely and explicitly given your consent

How long GEO keeps your personal data

When you begin an application, or return to an existing application you will be sent an email with a pin number.

If you do not use the pin to verify your email within 24 hours, you will need to start either the application or the log-in again and request a new pin.

Once you have started your application, we will keep your personal data for six calendar months from the date you last signed in. If you do not sign in within six calendar months we will permanently delete all personal identifiable data. We will warn you three months, one month and one week before we delete your data.

Once an application is submitted, this is shared with the HMCTS Gender Recognition administration team. They will use this data to process your application and put it to the Gender Recognition panel for review. Once it is shared with HMCTS - we hold the data for seven calendar days before deleting it permanently from our online system.

A legitimate business needs means:

  • That we have a need to retain your details in order to provide services to you or to your organisation
  • The law requires us to provide these services

Sharing your information

When you submit your application, your data will be shared with the HMCTS Gender recognition administration team. They require a secure login to access the data. The data is not shared with any third parties or government departments, agencies or arms length bodies. The Cabinet Office and it’s associated staff are also unable to access the application data stored on the system.

Before the application is submitted, the information stored is not shared at all with any person, department or organisation.

After your application is submitted, your information will be shared with our mail and email service providers where this is needed to process your application. It will also be shared with our hosting suppliers and third party IT support companies for the purposes of managing the site.

There are some cases when your information can be shared for other reasons, e.g. to prevent crime, respond to legal obligations or cooperate with public inquiries.

Your rights

  • You can ask to see and rectify the personal information GEO holds about you. Users can do this by logging into their application on the apply for a gender recognition certificate service
  • In certain circumstances, you may also object to or restrict the processing of your data by GEO by making a request to the grc-requests@cabinetoffice.gov.uk mailbox
  • If you have submitted your application, you will no longer be able to log in to access the apply for a gender recognition certificate service - but you can make a request within 7 calendar days to the grc-requests@cabinetoffice.gov.uk mailbox for us to amend/delete the data - after this time, you will need to contact the GRPenquiries@justice.gov.uk mailbox
